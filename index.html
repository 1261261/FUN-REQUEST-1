What is Formula One?

Formula One (F1) is the most prestigious international open-well, single-seat racing competition. 20 drivers (2 drivers per team) come from all around the world and race on tracks in many different countries.

The Season

Each season has around 19-24 Grand Prix races. The season usually starts in March and ends in December. The 2025 season had 24 races, and each race is about 300 kilometers long.

Race Weekend Time Line

  1. Friday: Practice sessions to learn the track and adjust the car
  2. Saturday: Qualifying to decide starting positions
  3. Sunday: Race day!

Some weekends also include a sprint race, which is a shorter race that gives extra points.

Point System

Points are awarded to the top 10 drivers of each race.

  1. 25 points
  2. 18 points
  3. 15 points
  4. 12 points
  5. 10 points
  6. 8 points
  7. 6 points
  8. 4 points
  9. 2 points
  10. 1 points

During sprint races, only the top 8 drivers earn points. First place gets 8 points, and each position after gets one less point, down to 1 point for 8th place.

Championships

Drivers and teams compete to win:

  • World Drivers' Championship (WDC)
  • World Constructors' Championship (WCC)
